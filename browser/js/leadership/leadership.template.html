Kinetic Global Leadership


Executive Committee

Jessica Bernheim - Co-founder, Chair of the Board


Jessica Bernheim co-founded the first Kinetic chapter at Williams College in 2012 during her freshman year at college. While at Williams, she served as campus director and helped Kinetic grow into one of the largest and most popular student organizations on campus. Jessica also led two teams working on food insecurity. Her first team designed and implemented the Suspended Groceries initiative, a partnership between food pantries and supermarkets, and her second team designed the Roots Rising program, an after-school and summer program that engages high school students in the intersection of social justice, community development and food and agriculture. She graduated cum laude from Williams College in 2016 with a degree in History. Jessica works as an Associate Consultant with Parthenon-EY in New York. When she’s not working, she can be found baking and talking about politics.

Bryan Jones - Co-founder, Executive Director


Bryan Jones helped build the first Kinetic chapter at Williams College from 15 students to over 65 active members with five successfully implemented projects. While at Williams, he spent a year and a half as campus director and led a team on building the entrepreneurship ecosystem in New England. He graduated from Williams College in 2016 with a degree in Statistics and Economics. Bryan works as an Associate Consultant with Bain & Company in Boston and has experience in international development. When he’s not working, he can be found attempting to cook dishes from countries that he has traveled to.

Robert Heckart - Secretary


Robert is currently a fellow in Harvard’s 2015 Advanced Leadership Initiative working on the public policy issue of children who are homeless alone. From August 2013 to January 2015, he was Senior Counsel to US Senator Carl Levin and worked on tax, banking, securities and capital markets legislation and regulatory issues and investigations by the Senate Permanent Subcommittee on Investigations. From December 2010 to August 2013, Robert was Senior Adviser for Economic and Financial Policy to US Senator Kirsten Gillibrand. Robert was a partner in the banking group at Davis Polk & Wardwell LLP where he worked on a broad range of international and domestic banking, securities, structured finance and bankruptcy transactions. In 1990, he established the firm’s Environmental Practice Group and founded and edited the Environmental Journal of Law and Practice published by Warren Gorham and Lamont.

Board of Directors

Don MacKinnon - Board Member


Don is the co-founder and co-CEO of Milq, a cultural curation app and website that harnesses the power of curation to organize the worlds of music, film and culture. Previously, Don founded and was CEO of Hear Music, an innovative music retailer featuring the first CD-based listening stations. In 1999, Starbucks purchased Hear Music, and Don went on to serve as Vice President of Music and Entertainment at Starbucks – overseeing the creation of over 300 compilations, leading digital innovations for content delivery and launching a Hear Music label that released Ray Charles’ Grammy winning, triple-platinum final album as well as Bob Dylan’s first recordings. Don then helped build Product (RED), the organization founded by Bono, dedicated to defeating AIDS through partnerships with iconic brands including Apple, Nike and the Gap. Don oversaw (RED)’s entertainment initiatives, in which artists ranging from Jay-Z to the Black Keys turned their music (RED). In addition to being on the advisory board of the Cooper Hewitt National Design Museum, Don has advised a number of entities on digital strategy including U2 and Ken Burns. In 2014, Don led the creation of the award-winning Ken Burns iPad app.
