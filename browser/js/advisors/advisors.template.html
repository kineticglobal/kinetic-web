Our Advisory Network

Meet our panel of experts on Environmental Sustainability, Homelessness, and Food Insecurity. These are professionals working in these fields, who offer their time and knowledge to our Kinetic teams. With their help, we are able to identify Kinetic-sized gaps around which teams can design and implement creative, sustainable and scalable solutions.

Mark Orlowski


Mark is the founder and executive director of the Sustainable Endowment Institute, where he heads its research efforts on college sustainability initiatives including creation of the College Sustainability Report Card. Mark also serves as the lead developer and coordinator of the Billion Dollar Green Challenge, an initiative that is encouraging colleges and other nonprofit institutions to invest a combined total of $1 billion into energy efficiency upgrades.

Dana Bourland


Dana is Vice President of the Environment Program at The JPB Foundation. Dana is leading the creation and development of the program with a goal to enable resilient communities across the US. Formerly Dana was Vice President of Green Initiatives for Enterprise Community Partners where she led environmental strategy for the national organization.

Matthew Aronson


Matthew Aronson is a homeless programs specialist and subject matter expert in youth homelessness and coordinated entry processes for the Office of Special Needs Assistance Programs at the US Department of Housing and Urban Development.

Sam Greenberg and Sarah Rosenkrantz


Sam and Sarah are Co-Founders of Y2Y Harvard Square, the nation's first student run shelter for young adults ages 18-24.

Ali Bloch


Ali is an experienced strategist and principal with over 15 years demonstrated success building early-stage ventures, shaping partnerships between diverse stakeholders, driving internal change initiatives and leading multi-disciplinary teams with a commitment to diligence, creativity and humor. Her content expertise includes health, technology, education, agriculture and social enterprise.
