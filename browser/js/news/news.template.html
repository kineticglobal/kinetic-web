Suspended Groceries

Suspended Groceries was the first Kinetic initiative. Aimed at bringing fresh, healthy food to food-insecure individuals, Suspended Groceries allows shoppers to pay for but not take groceries. Food pantries then distribute un-stigmatized coupons for these “suspended” groceries.

Roots Rising

Roots Rising is an initiative designed and implemented by a Sustainable Agriculture Kinetic team at Williams College in partnership with a Massachusetts’s based non-profit called Alchemy Initiative. Root’s Rising is an agriculture-based youth development program that builds community and empowers young people. Roots Rising pays teens to work on farms, in community gardens, and in local food pantries. It creates opportunities for teens to engage in meaningful work and leaves youth feeling purposeful, capable, and connected.

Opioid Buyback Program:

The Williams Kinetic team working on addressing the opioid epidemic in Western Massachusetts. Williams’ Kinetic team launched their pilot program this spring in partnership with the Adams, MA police department. The pilot is a “buy-back” program aimed at reducing opioid-related deaths in Western Massachusetts. Initial findings from the pilot are promising, and other police departments in the region have been in contact with the team about introducing similar programs in their communities.

Penn Closet

The University of Pennsylvania Kinetic team implemented a second-hand store on the Penn campus to address issues of sustainability that arise when students discard clothing or products at the end of the academic year. Founded on the principles of “reduce, reuse, recycle,” Penn Closet will help divert perfectly usable items from landfills while encouraging students to think differently about consumerism and waste.
